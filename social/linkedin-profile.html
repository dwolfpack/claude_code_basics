LinkedIn Profile

LinkedIn Profile Visibility Beginner

Your LinkedIn profile is your professional front door. A well-optimised profile gets 21× more views and 36× more recruiter messages. Here's everything you need to make yours stand out.

Headline — your most searchable field

  • Include your role + top skills with keywords recruiters search for: "Full-Stack Developer | React · Node.js · AWS"
  • Avoid vague phrases like "seeking opportunities" — be specific about your specialisation
  • Your headline appears everywhere: search results, connection requests, comments — make it count

Profile photo & banner

  • Profiles with a photo get 21× more views and 36× more messages — this is the single highest-ROI action you can take
  • Use a clean, well-lit headshot where your face fills most of the frame
  • Customise your banner with a simple graphic showing your tech stack or role — it reinforces your brand on every profile visit

About / Summary section

  • Write 2–3 focused paragraphs: your niche, what you build, and what drives you
  • Weave in a short narrative — what got you into tech, what problems you love solving
  • Naturally include keywords from job postings in your target role ("cloud infrastructure", "API design", "React")
  • Close with a soft call to action — "Open to exciting projects" or "Let's connect"

Experience — show outcomes, not just titles

  • For each role, describe projects you shipped, technologies used, and quantifiable outcomes
  • Use bullet points with numbers: "Reduced API response time by 40%", "Shipped feature used by 50k users"
  • Include personal projects and freelance work — all real-world experience counts
  • Link to GitHub repos, live demos, or articles directly from experience entries

Skills & Featured sections

  • Add a mix of languages, frameworks, tools, and methodologies relevant to your goals
  • Pin your top 3 most relevant skills — they appear first before "Show all"
  • Take LinkedIn Skill Assessments to earn verified badges — these surface in recruiter skill-based search filters
  • Use the Featured section to pin your best GitHub repos, live projects, or articles — it is highly visible and widely ignored by most profiles (your advantage)

Recommendations & Creator Mode

  • Ask teammates, managers, or professors for written recommendations shortly after completing a project — specificity matters more than seniority
  • Give endorsements to get endorsements — reciprocation is common
  • Turn on Creator Mode to add topic hashtags for better discoverability and unlock profile analytics
  • Completing your profile to "All-Star" status makes you 40× more likely to receive opportunities via LinkedIn's algorithm

Try this prompt

"I'm a junior developer applying for front-end roles. Here is my current LinkedIn About section: [paste text]. Rewrite it to be more compelling, keyword-rich, and specific. Include the fact that I build projects with Claude Code. Keep it under 300 words."
