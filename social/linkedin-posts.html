Writing LinkedIn Posts

LinkedIn Content Storytelling Intermediate

A single well-written LinkedIn post about a project you built can reach thousands of people — including hiring managers. Here's how to write posts that get read, shared, and remembered.

Hook first — the 110-character rule

  • LinkedIn truncates posts after roughly 110 characters with a "…see more" cutoff — your first line must grab attention before readers ever click
  • Lead with a result, a surprising fact, or a relatable pain point — not "I'm excited to share that I built..."
  • Strong openers: "I spent 3 days debugging one line of code. Here's what I learned." or "Built my first API in 48 hours. Here's what actually broke."

Three storytelling frameworks that work

  • SLA — Story, Lesson, Application: what happened → what I learned → how you can use it
  • PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution: name the pain → make it relatable → show how you solved it
  • VSQ — Value, Story, Question: deliver a tip → back it with your experience → end with a question to drive comments
  • Pick one framework per post and stick to it — posts that jump between all three feel scattered

Structure for scanability

  • Use short paragraphs — 1 to 2 sentences max. LinkedIn is mobile-first; dense blocks lose readers instantly
  • Use bullet points with • or → and white space between sections
  • Optimal post length in 2025: 800–1,000 characters. Long-form articles work when the topic genuinely needs depth
  • Aim to leave each paragraph on a partial thought that pulls readers to the next line

Specificity beats enthusiasm

  • Do not just say "I built a web app." Say: "Built a weather dashboard in React — first load dropped from 4s to 1.2s after I added lazy loading and memoised the API calls."
  • Name the technologies, describe the challenge, quantify the outcome
  • Share what surprised you — it signals genuine experience, not just following a tutorial
  • Honest reflections on failure outperform polished success announcements every time

Visuals & hashtags

  • Posts with images get 2× more comments; videos get 5× more engagement — add a screenshot, screen recording, or before/after diagram
  • Even a clean code-snippet image performs better than text alone
  • Use 3–5 hashtags at the bottom only — more than 5 triggers LinkedIn's spam filter
  • Mix broad + niche: #SoftwareEngineering + #ReactJS. Use PascalCase (#MachineLearning not #machinelearning)

Timing & engagement

  • Best days and times: Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11 AM local time
  • The LinkedIn algorithm in 2025 prioritises early comment velocity — more comments in the first 60–120 minutes = much wider distribution
  • End every post with a genuine question to drive early engagement
  • Reply to every comment in the first hour — comment-reply interactions are a high-quality signal to the algorithm

What to post about your Claude Code projects

  • A challenge you gave Claude and what surprised you about the result
  • A feature you built with AI assistance — what the workflow looked like step by step
  • A performance optimisation with before/after metrics and the prompt you used
  • An honest review of what Claude Code does well and where it falls short
  • A mistake you made, how Claude helped you debug it, and what you learned
