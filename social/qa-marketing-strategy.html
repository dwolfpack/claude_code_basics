QA Manager Marketing Strategy

Career QA Management Israel Hi-Tech Intermediate

A research-backed personal marketing plan for QA managers targeting Israeli and global IT companies. The product is you. The customers are hiring managers, CTOs, and VPs of Engineering.

Your core brand position

"QA leader who bridges quality craft and business outcomes — someone who builds automation infrastructure AND culture, and speaks both engineer and executive."

This is rare. Most QA people are either deep technical executors or pure process managers. Being fluent in both, with AI-testing awareness on top, puts you in the top 10% of candidates.

What IT companies require in 2026

Non-negotiable baseline:

  • 6–10 years QA experience, with 3–5+ in a leadership role
  • Test automation fluency — Playwright is now #1, overtaking Selenium
  • Agile / DevOps / CI-CD integration ownership
  • AI-in-testing awareness — companies actively filter for this
  • Business impact metrics: defect leakage, release quality, time-to-market
  • ISTQB Advanced Level – Test Manager (CTAL-TM) — ATS systems scan for it

Director / Head of QA differentiators:

  • Shift-left testing strategy ownership from requirements stage
  • Cross-functional influence across product, dev, and DevOps
  • Culture-of-quality mindset, not just process
  • Strategic thinking that speaks executive language (cost, risk, velocity)
  • AI fluency — knowing where AI helps and where humans must lead

Phase 1 — Weeks 1–2

Foundation: LinkedIn profile overhaul

  • Headline formula: QA Manager | [Top 2–3 tools] | [Domain: e.g. Fintech / SaaS / Scale-ups]
    Example: "QA Manager | Playwright · CI/CD · AI Testing | Building quality cultures in scale-up environments"
  • About section: Lead with your biggest measurable win, then describe your quality philosophy. Close with a soft call to action.
  • Skills — cluster tightly: Playwright, Selenium, Test Automation, ISTQB, CI/CD, Agile, Shift-Left Testing, AI Testing. Remove legacy or unrelated skills.
  • Turn on Creator Mode for algorithmic visibility boost
  • Request 2–3 specific recommendations — ask contacts for concrete examples, not general praise
  • Pin your best content in the Featured section — a framework demo, an article, or a GitHub repo link
Phase 2 — Weeks 1–2

GitHub profile review & optimization

Israeli tech companies — especially startups — actively check GitHub when evaluating QA managers. It signals you are hands-on, not just a process manager.

  • Review all public repos — archive or delete anything that looks abandoned
  • Create a profile README (repo named exactly after your username) — bio, stack, links to LinkedIn
  • Pin 4–6 repos that represent your QA/automation work: test frameworks, CI/CD configs, BDD setups, Playwright suites
  • Every pinned repo needs a quality README: what it does, setup steps, example output or screenshot
  • Enable "Include private contributions on my profile" in settings — your company work shows as activity without exposing code
  • Add a short, professional bio and your location (Israel) in profile settings
  • Link your LinkedIn URL in the GitHub profile

What to pin as a QA manager:

  • A Playwright or Selenium test automation framework
  • A CI/CD pipeline config (GitHub Actions, Jenkins) with quality gates
  • Any test data generation, mock services, or BDD feature files
  • Scripts that demonstrate scripting/tooling skills (Python, JS)
Try this prompt

"Write a professional GitHub profile README for a QA Manager with 8+ years of experience. Specialisations: test automation with Playwright, CI/CD pipelines, shift-left testing, AI-assisted QA. Include a short bio, tech stack badges, what I'm currently working on, and links to LinkedIn. Keep it clean and skimmable."

Phase 5 — Ongoing

Network & inbound

  • Comment strategically: Thoughtful (not "great post!") comments on posts from QA Directors, CTOs, and VPs of Engineering at target companies — this drives profile visits
  • Facebook QA Groups: Join "QA Israel," "Hi-Tech Jobs Israel," and Hebrew-language QA groups. Be helpful, answer questions — referrals follow visibility
  • Ministry of Testing community: The most active global QA community — where senior QA hiring managers also hang out
  • Write one article per month: dev.to, Medium, or LinkedIn Articles. Topics: "3 QA metrics your CTO actually cares about," "Shift-left in practice," "How I reduced flaky tests by 60%"
  • Target 15–20 companies: Follow their engineering blogs, engage with their content before applying — when a role opens, you're a known name, not a cold application

Speaking opportunities (for later phases):

  • Local QA meetups and ISSTA (Israel software testing community)
  • Submit to EuroSTAR, Agile Testing Days, QA Global Summit
  • Podcast targets: TestGuild, Quality Bits, AB Testing Podcast
